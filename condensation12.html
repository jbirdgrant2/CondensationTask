Here is the scientific answer:

First, there are a lot of water molecules in the air, although we cannot see them.

Second, when the cold bottle was put on the table, the temperature near the bottle is much lower than the room temperature. Due to this lower temperature, the water molecules near the bottle move closer to each other to bond together. When water molecules bond together, we observe liquid water outside of the bottle at the macroscopic scale.

The scientific idea we used to answer Mike's question is called condensation. Condensation is a process in which gaseous water (water vapor) turns into liquid water when temperature drops. In this process, water molecules do not change into other types of molecules, but they move much closer to each other to bond together. When water molecules bond together, we observe liquid water at the macroscopic scale.
